HEIRLOOM PAINTING & FINISHING

What is HEIRLOOM Painting?

Our “Heirloom Painting” process is a system of sanding and preparing the surfaces that will remove the brush and roller marks from previous applications. This is accomplished by using a HEPAA certified dust extraction system and special sanding equipment which captures 98% of the dust generated during the sanding process. Drywall repairs are also sanded with the same results, and with virtually no dust residue.

We are the difference between applying new color to a surface and applying a new finish to the same surface. A value added benefit that is realized by this process is that once the surfaces have been prepared and finished using this Heirloom method, it is likely that the next time a color change is desired, preparation sanding will not be needed. This saves you $$$ and maintains a quality finished surface.

Each time a surface gets painted, the brush and roller texture left will amplify with each coat applied. During the third coat of finishing, the surfaces will have the appearance of “orange peel” effect. You will notice that the areas that have been patched, such as holes and dents in the wall surface appear to have a smooth and flat texture, while the surrounding area has the rough “orange peel”. You may also notice that the rough surface of the walls will catch and hold dust that is visible when the sun shines into the room, so much so that you now have cobb webs forming on the wall, especially near the ceiling or near fans.

If you have Millwork that is original to your home that has many layers of paint applied over the years, you will notice that the edges are now rounded over from the many layers of paint. We can resurface this original millwork to restore the original line crispness and flat surface qualities.
